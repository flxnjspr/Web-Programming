Season 1

Rotten Tomatoes gave season one a score of 89% based on 57 reviews, with the consensus reading: "Led by the surprisingly effective pairing of Andy Samberg and Andre Braugher, Brooklyn Nine-Nine is a charming, intelligently written take on the cop show format."

Season 2

Season 2 received a perfect 100% score from Rotten Tomatoes, based on 17 reviews. The consensus: "Brooklyn Nine-Nine's winning cast, appealing characters, and wacky gags make it good comfort food."

Season 6

Season 6 earned a remarkable 100% on Rotten Tomatoes with 27 reviews, and an 81 on Metacritic based on 9 reviews, indicating strong approval for its continued excellence.

Alyssa Rosenberg of The Washington Post deemed the show "one of the funniest, most important shows on TV," commending its ability to address issues in contemporary policing while keeping the humor intact.

Grace Robertson from Vanity Fair praised the series as a "well-made exemplar of the workplace sitcom," while Aisha Harris from Slate acknowledged its talented cast, despite noting the series' "hardly any discernible connection to current cultural attitudes about law enforcement."

The series has also been praised for its portrayal of LGBTQ characters, notably Captain Raymond Holt, an openly gay, no-nonsense black man, as well as Rosa Diaz coming out as bisexual in episodes "99" and "Game Night." These moments have been lauded for representing sexual orientation in a positive and groundbreaking way.

Ranking and Influence

Rolling Stone ranked Brooklyn Nine-Nine #24 on their list of "50 Best TV Shows of the 2010s," highlighting the "comedic yin and yang" of Samberg and Braugher's characters.
